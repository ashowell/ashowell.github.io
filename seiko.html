Project Overview

The task was to redesign a product of your choice for development, production, customer use, and disposal.

The Seiko SNK809 is a beginner level, 36mm field watch with Seiko's in house automatic 7S26 calibre movement. Field watches are typically used in outdoor or military settings where accuracy and precision are vital. While this watch is a "men's" watch, it is highly popular among women due to its small case size.

The main design for this project is seen on the left. The alternate design is seen below and breifly described in the "Main Design: Sustainability" section.

Main Design (above)

  • Quality and Reliability:
    By increasing the water resistance from 30m (splash proof) to 50m (swim proof), the watch more durable and reliable in situations where field watches are typically used.
  • Machining:
    By creating an entirely matte finish, the case can be fully machined and fully sandblasted without the need of extra steps.
  • Usability:
    Switching from traditional spring bars to quick release makes it easier for everyone to change and adjust the straps without the need for special tools.
  • Inclusivity:
    By shifting the starting point of the holes on the strap, the watch is more fitted for women who typically have smaller wrists.
  • Sustainability:
    Using recycled nylon for the strap decreases the environmental impact of the watch as the strap is usually the first part of the product to be thrown out. The alternate design (right) addresses This by excluding a strap entirely and handing the choice of strap to the buyer.
